# Observation Guide

Use this guide to compare publication results without overreading noisy platform
metrics.

## Checkpoints

Observe a new publication at the next scheduled patrol, then revisit it when a
prior observation requests a checkpoint or new activity appears. For fast-moving
platforms, an early and a 24-72 hour observation are often useful. For durable
blogs, later citations and search discovery may matter more than first-day
reactions. These are heuristics, not required timers.

## Performance Dimensions

Keep four dimensions separate:

- **Reach:** impressions, views, unique viewers, or visible distribution.
- **Engagement:** reactions, saves, comments, replies, reposts, and quotes.
- **Depth:** technical questions, corrections, substantive disagreement,
  click-through when visible, and evidence that readers used the artifact.
- **Durability:** later backlinks, citations, search discovery, renewed
  discussion, and continued traffic when visible.

A high-reach post can have little depth, while a low-volume technical discussion
can produce the most valuable feedback. Do not collapse them into one score.

## Comparison Rules

- Compare within the same platform, account, format, and similar observation
  window when possible.
- Treat unavailable metrics as unknown, not zero.
- Preserve exact observation times because platform counters continue changing.
- Look for direction and repeated patterns before claiming a format or topic
  performs better.
- Separate distribution effects from content effects. Account size, posting
  time, link treatment, and platform format can change reach independently of
  article quality.
- Never infer reader sentiment from reaction counts alone.

## Conversation Value

Prioritize a response when it corrects a factual error, answers a concrete
technical question, resolves confusion affecting other readers, adds missing
evidence, or opens a useful technical exchange. A response is usually
unnecessary for generic praise, repeated promotion, off-topic debate, or a
question already answered clearly in the thread.

When disagreement is credible, preserve it as potential counterevidence for
`eunomia-research-report`. Do not turn every objection into defensive copy.

## Compact Record

The daily-log entry should contain only:

- canonical or platform URL
- observation timestamp
- meaningful change since the previous observation
- important discussion or external echo
- recommended next action
- next checkpoint if one is useful

Do not copy full comment threads, analytics screenshots, or raw metric tables
into the repository.
